Army Worm

destructive caterpillar pest that damages crops

Solution: Regular monitoring, early detection, biological control (natural predators), and targeted pesticide application can effectively manage armyworm infestations.

  • Disease: Found
  • Category: Army Worm

bacterial blight

A severe bacterial disease causing leaf lesions, wilting, and significant yield losses, particularly in humid conditions.

Solution: practice crop rotation, and apply copper-based bactericides to manage bacterial blight effectively.

  • Disease: Found
  • Category: bacterial blight

aphids

Small sap-sucking insects that infest plants, causing stunted growth, leaf curling, and transmitting viruses, leading to reduced crop yields.

Solution: Introduce natural predators (like ladybugs), apply insecticidal soaps, and use reflective mulches to control aphid populations effectively.

  • Disease: Found
  • Category: aphids

powdery milldew

A fungal disease that appears as white, powdery spots on leaves and stems, leading to reduced plant vigor and yield.

Solution: Improve air circulation, apply fungicides, use resistant plant varieties, and avoid overhead watering to effectively control powdery mildew.

  • Disease: Found
  • Category: powdery milldew

Target Spot

A fungal disease that causes circular, dark lesions with concentric rings on leaves, leading to defoliation and reduced crop yields.

Solution: Use resistant varieties, ensure proper crop spacing, apply fungicides, and practice crop rotation to manage Target Spot effectively.

  • Disease: Found
  • Category: Target Spot
